# Dialogue And Voice

## Purpose

Use this file for 대화체, 말투, 캐릭터 음성, 카톡/문자, 학생 말투, 어른 말투, and dialogue repair.

## Dialogue Contract

Every dialogue line should shift at least one thing:

- status;
- information;
- threat;
- relationship;
- desire;
- misunderstanding;
- public reaction.

If a line only transfers information, consider turning it into a message, memo, status block, news line, or narration.

## Reference-Reading Findings

Effective webnovel dialogue is usually shorter than the narration around it. The line itself is plain, but the pressure comes from what the speaker wants, who is watching, and what the viewpoint character thinks but does not say.

Use the surface/inside contrast:

- Surface line: what the character can safely say.
- Inner line: what the viewpoint character actually thinks.
- Reaction line: what the room, enemy, ally, crowd, or chat does next.

This is especially useful for schemers, regressors, bullied students, workplace survivors, streamers, and protagonists hiding power.

## Speech Registers

### Teen Casual

- Short, plain, sometimes evasive.
- Use ordinary Korean: "야", "아니", "근데", "진짜", "뭐냐", "됐어".
- Avoid adult-like speeches, polished explanations, and long moral declarations.

Teen dialogue should sound like a person trying not to look too serious. Let embarrassment, avoidance, group pressure, and short jabs do the work.

### Adult Procedural

- Uses responsibility-avoidance language, confirmation, rules, schedules, reports, and institutional politeness.
- Good for teachers, managers, officials, doctors, lawyers, guild staff, and investigators.

Adult dialogue should often hide desire behind procedure: confirmation, policy, reports, appointments, safety, responsibility, or "for your sake" language.

### Rough Threat

- Shorter is stronger.
- Combine words with physical pressure: step closer, silence, blocking a path, grabbing a phone.
- Do not over-explain the threat.

### Cold Calculation

- Speaks in conditions, evidence, costs, probability, and leverage.
- Useful for schemers, regressors, tacticians, lawyers, merchants, and antagonists hiding emotion.

### Comic Light

- A joke must reveal character or relieve pressure briefly before the scene returns to tension.
- Do not let banter erase the chapter's danger or reward promise.

## Dialogue Repair

For each weak exchange:

1. Identify each speaker's hidden want.
2. Cut greetings and neutral confirmations.
3. Make one line carry pressure.
4. Give the listener a physical or social reaction.
5. End the exchange on a choice, reveal, insult, silence, or interruption.

## Group Chat And Messages

Use messages when speed, social pressure, evidence, or public reaction matters.

- Use `〈발신자〉 내용`.
- Use `〈모르는 번호〉` when sender identity should be hidden.
- Use `〈단톡방〉` for crowd reactions.
- Use `〈영상 자막〉` for captions attached to clips.

Keep messages short and dry. Do not wrap chat lines in quotation marks.

## Character Voice Checklist

- Can the reader tell who is speaking without a tag?
- Is the line natural for the speaker's age and status?
- Does the line create pressure or reveal motive?
- Does the line sound like spoken Korean, not a report?
- Is the strongest line placed near the end of the exchange?
