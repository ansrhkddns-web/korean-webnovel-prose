# Episode Engine

## Purpose

Use this file for 회차 설계, 회차 훅, 다음 화 클릭, 보상 루프, 초반 몰입, and retention repair.

## Episode Contract

Each episode should:

1. Resolve or complicate the previous hook early.
2. Apply immediate pressure.
3. Force the protagonist to choose or act.
4. Deliver one visible reward or reversal.
5. Leave one sharper unresolved pressure.

## Scene Beat Template

```text
Opening pressure:
Protagonist goal:
Obstacle:
Action:
Midpoint reward/reveal:
Cost or complication:
Ending object:
Next-click question:
```

## Reward Types

- Power: stat, skill, rank, technique, title, item.
- Information: name, rule, map, recording, witness, secret.
- Social: public reaction, status reversal, humiliation, recognition.
- Material: money, contract, territory, food, weapon, medicine.
- Emotional: confession, betrayal, apology, resolve, relationship change.
- Survival: safe route, shelter, antidote, escape, recovered body function.

## Strong Ending Buttons

- A name appears.
- A door opens.
- A number changes.
- A witness arrives.
- A message lands.
- A deadline locks.
- A reward reveals a cost.
- A public reaction flips.
- A monster or enemy crosses into a safe zone.
- The protagonist chooses the dangerous option.

## Weak Ending Patterns

- The character goes to sleep.
- The character decides to prepare.
- The scene ends on vague unease.
- The chapter explains a rule but changes nothing.
- A conflict ends and no new pressure appears.
- The ending depends on a mystery so vague the reader cannot form a question.

## Repair Moves

- Add a concrete object at the end.
- Move one reveal from the next chapter into the final page.
- Convert preparation into an irreversible action.
- Turn vague unease into a sound, smell, message, number, or witness.
- Add a cost to the reward.
- Make a third party see the wrong thing.

## Multi-Chapter Consistency

- Avoid two consecutive chapters with only setup.
- Avoid two consecutive chapters with only school/social pressure if the main promise is dungeon/system.
- Keep reward debt visible: if a promise is delayed, give a smaller adjacent payoff.
- Track what the reader is waiting for and pay one thing off every chapter or two.
